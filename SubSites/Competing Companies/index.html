AMD GPUs

  • Affordability

    • - Most AMD Hardware is much cheaper than the NVIDIA Alternatives

  • Open-Source Drivers

    • - AMD's drivers are open source, leading to better support, security, performance, and more updates, due to community patches and additions.

    - This also allows some users too create their own versions of the driver, for potentially problematic hardware that AMD may not support any longer.

  • Often behind

    • - Nvidia is much more focused on having the most powerful hardware compared too AMD, so if you need the best graphics card in the world you wont find it here.

Intel GPUs

WARNING: Intel Dedicated GPUs are extremely new, and currently alot about them is unknown!

  • Inbetween

    • - Intel seems to have a combination of problems & benefits between the two, for example they are kind of affordable, and decently powerful

  • Linux

    • Many benchmarks for Windows games on the new Intel Arc GPUs seem to often do better on Linux than their Windows counterparts.

  • Background

    • Intel GPUs do not have a good history, for Intel has always made CPUs, not GPUs, and some of their new performance benchmarks are... questionable due to their drivers.

NVIDIA GPUs

  • More powerful hardware

    • - NVIDIA almost always has more powerful hardware, being able to run most games at higher resolutions at ease.

  • NVIDIA APIs

    • - NVIDIA comes with many tools exclusive to their hardware.

  • Proprietary Software

    • - Most NVIDIA software is proprietary. This means that for Operating Systems like Linux, new useful tools like Wayland are almost completely innaccessible to NVIDIA Users, without some luck and sweat atleast.

  • Expensive

    • - NVIDIA hardware, especially newer ones, are extremely expensive.
