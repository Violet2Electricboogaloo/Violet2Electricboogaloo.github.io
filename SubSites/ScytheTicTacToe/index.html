A Utopia Isn't for all.

X

Why a Utopia isn't really to everyone's benefit.

We live in a world where we do not just get things because we wanted them. Everything we want or need has to be worked for, even if the person getting it isnt the one who was working for it. The idea of a perfect Utopia is corrupt, because for everything to be perfect, someone else has to be working for them perfectly. If we managed to create a utopia, there will almost always be someone underneath doing their hardest and best job to make it work. In the most likely scenario, since the higher ups of the world would not want to do it themselves, they will likely forceto do it for them. In the book Scythe, this is shown by the thunderhead and the scythes. For their life to be perfect, somebody has to manage life and their death. The job of handling life goes into the hands of the thunderhead, but since the humans believe that only humans would be able to decide who and how people should be killed to prevent overpopulation, that goes into the hands of scythes. In this fake utopia, the Scythes are truly experiencing a dystopia, with no humane way to solve it.

What Sacrifices are made to live forever?

X

What sacrifices are made to live forever?

The mind was never made to live for thousands of years, it was always made for smaller amounts. Although our life spans and our time on this earth has increased slowly, it has given us time to evolve and adapt. The sudden jolt to near perfect immortality comes with the drawbacks of the human mind. If nobody can die, and life never concludes, eventually life will get dull and repetitive. Seeing hundreds of thousands of years go by slowly can drive someone insane with no way to get out easily.
The aspect of Over Population also becomes an issue, because humanity needs recourses, and when humanity needs them they take them from nature, and give nature time to regrow, but as more and more people come into existance, there is not enough of those recourses from nature and no way to make them ourselves. Eventually, someone has to be killed, to help the ones who come after them. In the book Scythe, although we fail to get true representation of problems that couild potentially arise from immortality, we do get shown the sad solution for such a world. Their solution to overpopulation is killing off the ones they deem fit for death, but lots feel that with the ability of immortality that it is unfair. Within the age of mortality people would almost always know their moments before death, and also certain things such as murder feel unfair, things such as disease give you time before your death. When it comes to scythes, society, no matter the method, will never see it as reducing the population to prevent overpopulation, but always as just murder.
