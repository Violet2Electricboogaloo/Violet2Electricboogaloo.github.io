Honorable Scythe Faraday

Honorable Scythe Faraday was named after Michael Faraday, an English scientist and chemist who helped develop electricity as we know it.

Michael Faraday is one of the greatest scientists of the 19th century, starting out as a chemist. He wrote a manual that showed his mastery of very particular pieces of his science. He also discovered many new compounds such as benzene and liquified what people believed was a "permanent" gas. He made extreme contributions to the study of electromagnetism and electrochemistry, primarily in the field of electromagnetic induction. He was the one who established the basis for the electromagnetic fields. From there his studies helped develop a basis for electromagnetic rotary devices. Eventually, his efforts fully focused on the use of electricity for technology.
